Seoul Korea

Seoul, the vibrant capital of South Korea, is a dynamic blend of modernity and tradition. Known for its cutting-edge technology, bustling shopping districts, and rich history, the city offers something for every traveler. You can explore ancient palaces like Gyeongbokgung, stroll through the lively streets of Myeongdong, and experience the iconic views from N Seoul Tower. For those interested in culture, there are plenty of museums, traditional tea houses, and temples to visit. Don't miss the chance to indulge in Korean cuisine, with dishes like bibimbap, bulgogi, and the famous Korean barbecue. With its blend of old and new, Seoul is a must-visit destination for those seeking both adventure and cultural immersion.

Destinations

1. Gyeongbokgung Palace

The largest of the Five Grand Palaces in Seoul, it offers a stunning backdrop against the autumn foliage. The Changing of the Guard Ceremony is a must-see!

2. Namsan Seoul Tower (N-Seoul Tower)

Visit the observation deck for panoramic views of the city. In autumn, the view of the colorful foliage surrounding Namsan Mountain is breathtaking.

3. Karlovy VaryBukchon Hanok Village

Wander through this traditional Korean village, with its hanok (traditional houses), offering a glimpse of old Seoul amidst the modern city. In autumn, the village is surrounded by vibrant red and orange leaves.

Travel Tips

1. Explore the Outdoors

Autumn in Seoul is the perfect time to hike in one of the cityâ€™s many mountains, such as Bukhansan or Namsan. The fall foliage is absolutely stunning during hikes and outdoor walks.

2. Public Transportation

The subway and bus systems in Seoul are convenient and easy to navigate. Make sure to buy a T-money card for ease of travel across the city.

3. Check the Weather Forecast

While autumn in Seoul is typically crisp and clear, temperatures can fluctuate. Be sure to check the weather forecast regularly and bring an umbrella, as occasional rainfall can happen.
